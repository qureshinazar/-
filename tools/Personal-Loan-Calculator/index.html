📐 EMI Formula:
EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]

Where:
P = Principal Loan Amount
R = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
N = Total Number of Monthly Installments

🏠 vs 💰 Personal Loan vs Home Loan

  • Interest Rate — Personal loans have higher interest rates (10–24%) compared to home loans (7–10%).
  • Tenure — Personal loans are shorter (1–5 years) while home loans can go up to 30 years.
  • Collateral — Personal loans are unsecured (no collateral needed), whereas home loans are secured against the property.
  • Loan Amount — Personal loan amounts are generally lower (up to ₹40 lakhs) compared to home loans.
  • Processing Time — Personal loans are approved faster, often within 24–48 hours.

💡 Tips Before Taking a Personal Loan

  • Compare Rates — Always compare interest rates from multiple banks and NBFCs before choosing a lender.
  • Check Your Credit Score — A CIBIL score of 750+ helps you get better interest rates and faster approvals.
  • Borrow Only What You Need — Avoid over-borrowing as it increases your EMI burden and total interest cost.
  • Choose the Right Tenure — A shorter tenure means higher EMI but less total interest. A longer tenure reduces EMI but increases overall cost.
  • Read the Fine Print — Check for processing fees, prepayment penalties, and hidden charges before signing.
  • Plan for Prepayment — If possible, make prepayments to reduce the outstanding principal and save on interest.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How is Personal Loan EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the reducing balance formula: EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1], where P is the loan amount, R is the monthly interest rate, and N is the total number of monthly installments.

What is the typical interest rate for personal loans?

Personal loan interest rates in India typically range from 10% to 24% per annum, depending on the bank, your credit score, income, employment type, and loan amount.

What is the maximum tenure for a personal loan?

Most banks offer personal loans with a tenure of 12 to 60 months (1 to 5 years). Some lenders may offer up to 72 months depending on the borrower's profile.

Is my data safe?

Can I prepay my personal loan?

Most banks allow prepayment of personal loans, though some may charge a prepayment penalty (typically 2–5% of the outstanding amount). Check with your lender for specific terms.
