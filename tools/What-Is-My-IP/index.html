📖 What Is an IP Address?

An IP (Internet Protocol) address is a unique numerical label assigned to every device connected to the internet. Think of it as your device's "home address" on the web — it tells websites and online services where to send the data you requested.

There are two types of IP addresses:

  • Public IP — Assigned by your ISP, visible to the internet. This is what our tool shows.
  • Private IP — Used within your local network (e.g., 192.168.x.x). Not visible to external websites.

🤔 Why Would You Need to Know Your IP?

  • VPN Verification — Confirm that your VPN is working by checking if your IP has changed to a different location.
  • Troubleshooting — Network issues often require knowing your public IP for diagnostics.
  • Remote Access — Setting up remote desktop, servers, or CCTV often requires your public IP.
  • Security Checks — Verify if your IP has been exposed or blacklisted.
  • Gaming — Some multiplayer games or servers require your IP for direct connections.
  • Web Development — Developers often need to whitelist IPs for API access or testing.

🛡️ How to Hide Your IP Address

  1. Use a VPN — The most popular method. A VPN encrypts your traffic and assigns you a different IP from another location.
  2. Use a Proxy Server — Routes your traffic through an intermediary server, but doesn't encrypt it.
  3. Use Tor Browser — Routes traffic through multiple nodes for maximum anonymity, but is slower.
  4. Use Mobile Data — Switching from Wi-Fi to mobile data gives you a different IP address.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is an IP address?

An IP address is a unique numerical label assigned to every device connected to the internet. It serves as your device's identity, allowing websites and services to send data to and from your device.

What is the difference between IPv4 and IPv6?

IPv4 uses 32-bit addresses (e.g., 192.168.1.1) with about 4.3 billion possible addresses. IPv6 uses 128-bit addresses with virtually unlimited addresses. IPv6 was created as the world runs out of IPv4 addresses.

Can someone track me using my IP address?

An IP address can reveal your approximate location (city/region) and ISP, but it cannot pinpoint your exact physical address. Using a VPN can mask your real IP for added privacy.

How do I hide my IP address?

You can use a VPN (Virtual Private Network), a proxy server, or the Tor browser. A VPN is the most popular and reliable method.

Does my IP address change?

If your ISP assigns you a dynamic IP (most common), your IP may change periodically when you restart your router or periodically. A static IP stays the same and is usually used for servers or businesses.
