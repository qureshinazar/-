💡 Tips for a Perfect Passport Photo

  • Use a Plain Background — White or light-colored backgrounds work best for government forms.
  • Good Lighting — Ensure even, natural lighting on your face. Avoid harsh shadows.
  • Face the Camera — Look directly at the camera with a neutral expression.
  • No Accessories — Remove sunglasses, hats, and heavy jewelry (except religious headwear).
  • High Resolution — Upload the highest quality photo possible for best results after compression.
  • Crop Carefully — Make sure your face is centered and occupies 70–80% of the frame.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What size photo is needed for Indian Passport?

Indian Passport requires 3.5 x 4.5 cm (413 x 531 pixels at 300 DPI) with a plain white or light background. File size: 20–50 KB in JPEG.

How does auto-compression work?

The tool starts at your selected quality and tries to create the JPEG. If the file is too large, it reduces quality; if still too large, it scales down dimensions. If too small, it increases quality. This loops until the file is within the target range.

Can I change the target file size?

Yes! The default target is 20–50 KB (most government forms), but you can change the min and max values to any range you need.

What image formats are supported?

You can upload JPG, PNG, WEBP, BMP, or any browser-supported image format. The output is always JPEG.

Is my photo safe?

Yes! All processing happens locally in your browser. Your photo is never uploaded to any server or stored anywhere.

Is this tool free?

Yes, completely free. No sign-up, no watermarks, no limits.
