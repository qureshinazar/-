💡 What is a Moratorium Period? Most education loans offer a moratorium (grace period) = course duration + 6 to 12 months. During this time, no EMI is due, but interest keeps accruing and gets added to your principal. This means a longer moratorium increases your total repayment amount.

📐 EMI Formula:
EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]

Where:
P = Principal (Original Loan + Moratorium Interest, if applicable)
R = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate / 12 / 100)
N = Total Repayment Months (after moratorium)

💡 Tips Before Taking an Education Loan

  • Compare Interest Rates — Check rates from SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda, HDFC, and NBFCs. Government banks often offer lower rates.
  • Understand the Moratorium — Know exactly when repayment starts. Interest accrues during the moratorium and increases your total cost.
  • Check for Scholarships First — Apply for scholarships and grants before borrowing. Even partial funding reduces your loan burden.
  • Claim Tax Benefits — Interest paid on education loans qualifies for tax deduction under Section 80E for up to 8 years.
  • Consider Partial Payments — Paying even small amounts during the moratorium period reduces the interest that accumulates.
  • Read the Fine Print — Check for processing fees, prepayment penalties, and whether the rate is fixed or floating.
  • Plan for Living Expenses — Include accommodation, travel, books, and daily expenses in your loan estimate.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How is Education Loan EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the reducing balance formula. If you have a moratorium period, interest accrues during that time and is added to the principal before EMI calculation begins.

What is a moratorium period?

It's a grace period (typically course duration + 6–12 months) during which you don't pay EMIs. However, interest continues to accrue and gets added to the loan amount, increasing your total repayment.

What is the typical interest rate for education loans?

Education loan rates range from 7% to 12% for domestic studies and 8% to 14% for overseas studies, depending on the bank, loan amount, and collateral.

Can I get tax benefits on education loans?

Yes! Under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act, the interest paid on education loans is fully deductible (no upper limit) for up to 8 years from the year you start repaying.
