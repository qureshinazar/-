📐 EMI Formula:
EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]

Where:
P = Principal Loan Amount
R = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
N = Total Number of Monthly Installments (Years × 12)

💡 Tips to Reduce Your Home Loan EMI

  • Make a Larger Down Payment — A higher down payment reduces the loan amount and therefore the EMI.
  • Choose a Longer Tenure — Extending the tenure reduces your monthly EMI, though total interest increases.
  • Compare Interest Rates — Shop around and compare rates from multiple banks to find the best deal.
  • Opt for Floating Rate — Floating interest rates are usually lower than fixed rates and can save you money over the long term.
  • Make Prepayments — Whenever possible, make partial prepayments to reduce the outstanding principal and save on interest.
  • Maintain a Good Credit Score — A CIBIL score of 750+ can help you negotiate a lower interest rate.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How is Home Loan EMI calculated?

EMI is calculated using the standard reducing balance formula: EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1], where P is the loan amount, R is the monthly interest rate, and N is the total number of monthly installments.

What factors affect my Home Loan EMI?

Three main factors: (1) Loan Amount — higher amount means higher EMI, (2) Interest Rate — higher rate increases EMI and total interest, (3) Loan Tenure — longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid.

Is this Home Loan Calculator free?

Yes, completely free. No sign-up, no downloads — enter your details and get instant results.

Should I choose a longer or shorter tenure?

A longer tenure means lower monthly EMI but you pay more total interest. A shorter tenure means higher EMI but you save significantly on interest. Choose based on your monthly budget and financial goals.
