About the 8th Pay Commission

The 8th Central Pay Commission (8th CPC) was approved by the Union Cabinet to review and revise the pay structure of central government employees and pensioners across India. The recommendations are expected to cover:

  • Basic Pay revision using a fitment factor multiplier
  • Revised Dearness Allowance (DA) rates
  • Updated House Rent Allowance (HRA) slabs
  • Revised Transport Allowance (TA) and other allowances
  • Pension revision for retired government employees

Jammu & Kashmir holds a unique position as the first state to implement the 7th Pay Commission recommendations through SRO-193 dated 24th April, 2018. J&K government employees and pensioners can use this calculator to plan their finances ahead of the 8th CPC implementation.

What is a Fitment Factor?

A fitment factor is a multiplication factor used to determine the revised basic pay under a new pay commission. It is applied to the existing basic pay to arrive at the new basic pay. For example:

  • Under the 7th CPC, the fitment factor was 2.57.
  • Under the 6th CPC, the fitment factor was 1.86.
  • For the 8th CPC, the expected fitment factor ranges from 1.92 to 2.86, with most estimates around 2.28 to 2.57.

Formula: New Basic Pay = Current Basic Pay × Fitment Factor

Q1: What is the 8th Pay Commission?

The 8th Pay Commission is a government body constituted by the Union Cabinet to revise the salaries, pensions, and allowances of central and state government employees. It is expected to be implemented from January 1, 2026.

Q2: What is the expected fitment factor for the 8th Pay Commission?

While the exact fitment factor has not been officially announced, estimates range between 1.92 to 2.86. Common expectations suggest a fitment factor of around 2.28 to 2.57, which would significantly increase the basic pay of government employees.

Q3: How is the new basic pay calculated under the 8th Pay Commission?

The new basic pay is calculated by multiplying your existing basic pay (under 7th CPC) by the fitment factor. For example, if your current basic pay is ₹40,000 and the fitment factor is 2.50, your new basic pay would be ₹40,000 × 2.50 = ₹1,00,000.
